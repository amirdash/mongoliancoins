AV dinar. Temp. Ogodei Khan (1227-1241). Minted in Qorum (Qara Qorum , the capital city Great Mongol Empire, present day in Mongolia).
Obverse: At this point the inscription on this side has not been completely interpreted yet. Hovewer, the last line at the bottom reads the word “KHORM” (ХОРУМ) in mongol script for Qara Qorum as the place where the coin was minted. This inscription is similar to the ones on the coin of Ogodei and Guyug khans, which have Qara Qorum's name inscribed as “KHORM”. The reverse margin has date and mint, but mint, but the mint name is flan, and sections “fi shuhur sana thalatin ” - that means “in the months of the year 30”. This coin was minted approximately in 630AH/1230AD during Ogodei khan's reign.
Reverse: Kalima and name of the Caliph Nasir of Baghdad.

AR dirham Nusrat Al-Din Berke (1257-1267)
Bulghar mint. ND.
Citing the Great Mongol Khan Arigbuqa (657-662AH/ 1260-1264AD)
Obverse: ArigBuka / Khan Al a-zam
Reverse: Zarb / Tamgha / Bulghar
14mm, 0.77g, A-2019D. Singatullina ?10 There is much debate about whether Arigbuqa was actually a Great Khan of Mongolia. Whatever the case, there is proof that Arigbuqa was in fact the Great king of Mongolia in a coin and tamgha released in the 13th century. The coin and the tamgha were used in the Golden Horde and Chagatai Khanate. There are even reports of such coins with the same tamgha being found in Khara Khorum. There is no other way to interpret the words ““Ariqbuqa / Khan al-Azam /Al-Adl” “ on the coin other than “Arigbuqa The Great Khan.” There is simply no other meaning. The trident tamgha on the back of the coin is very similar to the tamgha on the coin of Arigbuqa’s older brother Möngke Khan. However, the trident tamgha on ari Khan’s coin is unique in that the exact same tamgha is not found on any other coin. This shows that the coin is specific to Arigbuqa Khan only. In the Great Khuraldai of 1260 that took place in (orkhonii river valley), Arigbuqa was made Khan of Mongolia. This appointment was approved by both the Golden Horde and the Chagatai Khanate. As a show of their approval the Golden Horde and Chagatai Khanate released this coin. This is the closest explanation to the truth. There is no other coin in the world that bears the Great Arigbuqa Khan’s name and tamgha making this coin a unique and special part of Mongolian history. 2018.GMH025
